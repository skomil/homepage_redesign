He joined the McKinsey & Company consulting firm and started Environmental Lighting Concepts (ELC) - installing energy efficient lighting systems - while there. After selling his company to Kansas City Power & Light, he eventually assumed management of KCP&L’s entire portfolio of competitive businesses. He grew the business more than tenfold, from less than $100 million dollars to almost a billion dollars in annual revenue. In 2004, Greg co-founded Denali Partners, LLC, and has been working to provide capital and management services to help small businesses grow since then. IN 2010, Greg co-founded the Common Sense Coalition. Greg is active in the American Legion’s Boys’ State program and has served on the boards of the Kansas City Zoo and the Gala at the Glen for Cystic Fibrosis. Greg now lives in Olathe with his wife, Sybil and their two dogs. {% endcapture %} {% capture content %} Independent Greg Orman is running against Republican incumbent Pat Roberts. Previously, this was a three-way race between Orman, Roberts and Democrat Chad Taylor. Taylor dropped out of the race on September 3rd due to lack of firm support. ##Why We Chose Greg Orman Greg Orman is a reformer who believes that Washington is broken and he is committed to ending big money's influence in politics. He called our current campaign finance system ["legalized bribery"](http://www.ormanforsenate.com/campaign_finance_reform), has an extensive reform platform on his campaign website, and favors a [constitutional amendment](http://www.ormanforsenate.com/campaign_finance_reform) to overturn Citizens United and passing [reform bills](http://www.ormanforsenate.com/campaign_finance_reform) like the American Anti-Corruption Act. ##About Kansas Senate Race MAYDAY.US will spend at least $1 million to elect Independent Greg Orman and defeat Republican Pat Roberts. The [Rothenberg Political Report](https://rothenbergpoliticalreport.com/ratings/senate) and the [Cook Political Report](http://cookpolitical.com/senate/maps) both rate the race as a toss-up.
